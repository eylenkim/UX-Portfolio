Project Overview

About Spotify: A music streaming platform, used mostly by gen-z and millenials. Well-known for their music recommendation algorithms, annual Spotify Wrapped campaigns, and social aspect.

Brief: Research, design, prototype, and test ways to answer the question “How can we utilize existing strengths to create engaging profile features?”

Timeline: 1.5 weeks

The Goal Design an engaging way for people to interact with their Spotify profiles

Role User Research / Design Conceptualization / UX + UI Design / Usability Testing / Empathy Mapping / Wireframing / Prototyping / Brand Identity / Iconography

Empathize

Problem Assumptions

  • Listeners enjoy learning about their friend’s music habits, but there aren’t enough features that help you gain insight
  • Spotify profiles do not satisfy curiosity and provide enough joy, therefore, users don’t have incentives to visit profiles
  • Profiles do not tell stories about the listener

Hypothesis

  • Provide users with more profile customization features
  • Create unique and engaging profile features that allows users to track their listening history

Survey

  • 60 participants revealed their profile-visiting habits and features they valued most in Spotify
  • The criteria: respondents must use Spotify regularly (at least 3 days in a week) and use the desktop app at least once a week

50% of the respondents landed on a friend’s profile by seeing their recently played songs in the Friend Activity list.

Key Learnings

  • Improving readability: Searching for destinations is information-heavy. Visual hierarchy, color contrast, and segmented control helps organize dense information and maintains simplicity for users who are browsing.
  • A bit of research goes a long way: I wish I had more bandwidth to research thoroughly; however, working within a time constraint, I learned that even a bit of research went far. Running a competitive analysis provided insight when it came to prioritizing features and branding/styling decisions.
