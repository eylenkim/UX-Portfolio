About

My approach with art is to interpret experiences and information into vivid illustrations. I encourage people to imagine life outside of their own perception — whether that's through culture, abstract shapes, or philosophy.

Tools: Pen + paper / Procreate / Adobe Photoshop

Culture

As a Soviet Korean, my art helps people think about a piece of history that is never taught in world history classes. During the Soviet Union, Stalin displaced groups of Korean people throughout Central Asia. This forced them to adopt a new culture, affecting future generations like me. This is why I speak fluent Russian! I am proud of representing my unique background through these pieces.

People

A collection of people I drew during quarantine. I wanted to learn something new, so I watched art lessons to pass the time. I learned about multiplying colors and using shapes to redefine what reality is. My goal was to apply these concepts and challenge myself to draw people of all ages and backgrounds.

Philosophy

Creating art revolving around philosophical theory is very challenging and that is why I gravitate towards it. It allows me to soak in the theory, ideate, and come up with a visual translation. I enjoy transforming existentialism and stoicism into art.
